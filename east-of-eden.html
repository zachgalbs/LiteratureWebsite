East of Eden

About the Book

East of Eden was written by John Steinbeck in 1952. This novel is about good vs evil, and highlights one character specifically, Cal, who is enlightened by the idea of freedom of choice, or timshel. There is a biblical allusion throughout the novel tying to the Cain and Abel, which is present in the names of characters that start with 'C' or 'A'

Author

The author of East of Eden, John Steinbeck, was an American author who was known for his immense story-telling skills. He combines horation and juvenalian satire to change the perspective of the reader on the behaviors of others. John Steinbedck is widely regarded as one of the great American novelists of the 20th century.

Key Themes

  • Good vs. Evil
  • Timshel, The freedom of choice
  • Family and Heritage
