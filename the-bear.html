The Bear

About the Story

The Bear is a short story by William Faulkner, written in 1942. This book focuses on Old Ben, a bear from the life of Ike McCaslin and his experiences hunting in his childhood. This work is part of a larger collection, "Go Down, Moses". The narrative talks about themes of nature, and the passage of time.

Author

William Faulkner was an American writer and Nobel Prize laureate from Oxford, Mississippi. He wrote lots of different forms of writing, and is known for his novels set in the fictional Yoknapatawpha County, based in Mississippi, where he spent most of his life. You should know that lots of the story is based on the life of Faulkner, and that part of the story is autobiographical. Faulkner is known for being hard to read.

Key Themes

  • Nature and the Wilderness
  • Idealization and fear of Old Ben
  • Coming of Age
