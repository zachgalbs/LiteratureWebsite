The Bear

About the Story

The Bear is a short story by William Faulkner, part of his collection "Go Down, Moses," published in 1942. The story revolves around a young boy named Ike McCaslin and his experiences hunting a legendary bear named Old Ben. The narrative delves into themes of nature, heritage, and the passage of time.

Author

William Faulkner was an American writer and Nobel Prize laureate from Oxford, Mississippi. Faulkner wrote novels, short stories, a play, poetry, essays, and screenplays. He is primarily known and acclaimed for his novels and short stories set in the fictional Yoknapatawpha County, based on Lafayette County, Mississippi, where he spent most of his life.

Key Themes

  • Nature and the Wilderness
  • Heritage and Legacy
  • Rite of Passage
