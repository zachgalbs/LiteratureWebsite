The Great Gatsby

About the Book

The Great Gatsby is a novel by F. Scott Fitzgerald, written in 1925. This novel is set in the Roaring 20s, and tells the story of Jay Gatsby's (or James Gats') love for Daisy Buchanan. This novel highlights the carelessness of the rich and Gatsby's focus on the past, which, as the book points out, isn't necessarily bad. The book ends dramatically with the death of Jay Gatsby and the lack of people who come to his funeral, specifically the absence of Daisy Buchanan, who loved Gatsby.

Author

Francis Scott Key Fitzgerald was an American novelist, essayist, screenwriter, and short-story writer. He is best known for his novels which show idealized aspects of the Roaring 20's. During his life, he published four novels, 164 short stories. You need to know that Fitzgerald had a heavy focus on satirizing the rich.

Key Themes

  • The American Dream
  • Love and Desire
  • Wealth and Class
  • Focus on the Past
