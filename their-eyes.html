Their Eyes Were Watching God

About the Book

Their Eyes Were Watching God is a novel by Zora Neale Hurston, published in 1937. The book tells the story of Janie Crawford and her journey to find her own identity and voice through three marriages. The novel explores themes of love, independence, and the search for self-fulfillment.

Author

Zora Neale Hurston was an American author, anthropologist, and filmmaker. She portrayed racial struggles in the early-20th-century American South and published research on hoodoo. The most popular of her four novels is Their Eyes Were Watching God, published in 1937.

Key Themes

  • Love and Independence
  • Search for Identity
  • Struggles of African American Women
