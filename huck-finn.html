The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn

About the Book

The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn is a novel written by Mark Twain in 1884. This book tells the story of Huck Finn, who escapes his abusive father and caretakers. He travels down the Mississippi River and meets a runaway slave named Jim. The two continue on many adventures by themselves. The novel addresses important themes such as race, freedom, and moral dilemmas that Huck faced in the South

Author

Mark Twain, the author of The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn, was born Samuel Langhorne Clemens, and was a famous American writer, humorist, and lecturer. He focused on the problems in society and how to solve them. He was credited as the greatest humorist the United States has produced and William Faulkner (another author we read about) called him the father of American literature.

Key Themes

  • Race and Slavery
  • Freedom and Adventure
  • Moral and Social Satirization
